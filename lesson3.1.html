Lesson 3

Coffee Roasting

Discover how green coffee beans transform into rich, aromatic beans through heat, time, and chemical change.

What Happens During Roasting?

Roasting is the process that transforms green coffee beans into the brown, aromatic beans we use to brew coffee. As heat is applied, the beans go through physical and chemical changes.

The transformation begins

During roasting, coffee beans react to heat in several ways. Their color changes, moisture evaporates, sugars caramelize, and the aroma we know as coffee starts to appear.

This process is what gives roasted coffee its unique taste, smell, and final character in the cup.

Roasting changes everything

During roasting:

  • The beans change color from green to brown.
  • Moisture inside the beans evaporates.
  • The beans expand and become lighter in weight.
  • Natural sugars begin to caramelize.
  • Oils move toward the surface.
  • Flavor and aroma start to develop.
