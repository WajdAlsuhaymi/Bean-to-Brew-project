Lesson 5

Brewing Methods

Explore how different brewing methods shape coffee’s flavor, body, and strength — and why the same beans can create completely different experiences.

Brewing is the final step in making coffee. It is the process where water extracts flavor from ground coffee and turns it into the drink we enjoy. The brewing method you choose can greatly affect taste, strength, and body.

Brewing Styles

Each method reveals coffee in a different way.

Percolation Brewing

Water passes through the coffee grounds and filters down, creating a clean, lighter cup with more clarity and delicate notes.

Clean & balanced

Immersion Brewing

Coffee grounds stay in water for a longer time, giving the cup more body, deeper flavor, and a smoother texture.

Full body

Pressure Brewing

Water is pushed through the coffee quickly under pressure, producing a bold, concentrated, and intense taste.

Bold & rich
“Different styles extract flavor in different ways.”
