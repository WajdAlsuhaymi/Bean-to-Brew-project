Coffee Grinding

Discover how grind size changes extraction, flavor, bitterness, and the way water moves through coffee.

What Happens if you use the wrong size?

Using the wrong grind size can change the entire brewing result. A grind that is too fine may slow the flow too much and create over-extraction, while a grind that is too coarse may allow water to pass too quickly and cause under-extraction.

Fine

Usually used for espresso. Water moves more slowly and extraction becomes stronger.

Medium

A balanced grind size, commonly used for drip brewing methods and many filter coffees.

Coarse

Best for methods like French press, where water stays in contact with coffee longer.
