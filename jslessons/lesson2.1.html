Lesson 2

Types of Beans

Discover how different coffee beans shape flavor, aroma, body, and caffeine level — and why every bean tells a different story.

Do all coffee beans taste the same?

Coffee is more than just a bitter drink. Each bean has its own identity, shaped by origin, species, roasting style, and growing environment. These differences help professionals describe coffee more clearly and allow coffee lovers to understand why one cup feels fruity and elegant, while another feels bold and intense.

A quick look into the world of coffee beans.

The two most famous coffee beans in the world

Arabica and Robusta are the most recognized coffee beans globally. Each one offers a different experience in flavor, strength, and character.

Arabica

Smooth & Elegant

Arabica is the most popular coffee type worldwide. It is loved for its smooth and layered flavor, often showing fruity, floral, or slightly sweet notes with a gentle acidity.

Most widely consumed coffee bean in the world.
More delicate, aromatic, and complex in flavor.
Contains less caffeine than Robusta.
🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia & Arabic Coffee

A cup rooted in culture

Saudi Arabia has a deep and meaningful relationship with Arabic coffee. In the southern region of the Kingdom, especially in Jazan, coffee is grown on mountain terraces, and Saudi Khawlani coffee is celebrated for its unique taste and cultural value.

Arabic coffee is often lightly roasted and flavored with cardamom. More than a drink, it represents hospitality, generosity, warmth, and a strong sense of heritage in Saudi culture.

Bold & Powerful

Robusta is known for its strong body, intense taste, and higher caffeine level. It is often chosen when a richer, heavier, and more bitter profile is needed in the cup.

Stronger and more bitter than Arabica.
Contains more caffeine and produces heavy body.
Commonly used in espresso blends and commercial coffee.

Robusta

🌿 Vietnamese Robusta

Vietnamese Robusta is one of the most famous examples of Robusta coffee in the world. It is known for its powerful taste, noticeable bitterness, and high caffeine content. Because of its strength and thick crema, it is often used in espresso blends and many commercial coffee products.
