Roast Levels

Explore how roasting changes sweetness, acidity, bitterness, body, and the final personality of coffee.

How Roasting Changes Coffee

Sweetness can become deeper
Bitterness becomes stronger
Acidity becomes softer
Body becomes heavier
Original origin flavors become less noticeable

Roasting shapes the personality of coffee

If roasting changes flavor this much, how different are light and dark roasts?

Roast Levels

Light Roast

  • Light brown color
  • Higher acidity
  • Original bean flavors remain clear
  • Lighter body

Medium Roast

  • Medium brown color
  • Balanced acidity and bitterness
  • Smooth and well-rounded flavor
  • Medium body

Dark Roast

  • Dark brown color
  • Lower acidity
  • Stronger bitterness
  • Heavier body and bold smoky notes

As roasting increases:

  • Acidity decreases
  • Bitterness increases
  • Body becomes heavier

Roasting is the stage where coffee truly comes to life. From light to dark, each roast level creates a different balance of acidity, bitterness, and body. Understanding these differences helps you choose the roast that best matches your taste.
