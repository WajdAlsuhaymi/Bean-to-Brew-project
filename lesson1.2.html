What’s Inside the Coffee Cherry?

Before coffee becomes a drink, it begins as a fruit. Let’s explore the layers inside the coffee cherry and discover where the coffee journey really starts.

Inside the Coffee Cherry

Each coffee cherry contains several layers. These layers protect the seed and support its growth before it becomes the coffee bean we know.

The Skin

The outer skin is the first visible layer. It protects the fruit and turns red when the cherry becomes ripe.

→

The Pulp

The pulp is the soft fruit around the seed. It adds moisture and surrounds the beans inside the cherry.

→

The Seeds

These seeds are the beginning of coffee beans. After processing and roasting, they become the coffee used for brewing.

Coffee Farms — Where the Journey Begins

Coffee plants need the right environment to grow well. Climate, rain, altitude, and soil all shape the final flavor of coffee.

🌱 Where Do Coffee Plants Grow?

Coffee plants grow best in warm regions near the equator, where the weather stays balanced and the soil supports healthy crops.

  • Mild temperatures
  • Regular rainfall
  • High altitudes
  • Rich fertile soil

These special regions are often called the Coffee Belt.

Coffee grows best in the Coffee Belt

Lesson Wrap-Up

Coffee begins as a fruit, not as a drink. Inside each coffee cherry, the seeds wait to be processed and transformed into coffee beans.

By understanding the layers of the cherry and the places where coffee grows, we get closer to understanding every cup we brew.
