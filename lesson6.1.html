Lesson 6

Find Your Coffee Match

You’ve explored coffee from bean to brew. Now it’s time to discover the type of coffee that truly matches your taste, mood, and daily routine.

You’ve explored coffee from bean to brew — learning about origins, roasting, grinding, and brewing. Now comes the final step of the journey: choosing the coffee that truly matches your taste. Understanding how to select coffee will help you turn knowledge into experience and make every cup more enjoyable.

A coffee bag is a guide to your cup

Origin

Shows where the coffee comes from and whether it is single origin or a blend.

Traceability

Information about the farm, cooperative, or region where the coffee was produced.

Date

The roasting date helps you know how fresh the coffee is and when it tastes best.

CO₂ Valve

Lets carbon dioxide out while protecting the coffee from oxygen that harms aroma.

Roast Level

Light, medium, or dark roast changes the flavor style and how the coffee feels in the cup.

Expected Taste

Tasting notes describe what flavors you may notice, like chocolate, citrus, nuts, or berries.

Find Your Style

Choose Your Coffee Personality

Different flavor profiles match different moods, preferences, and moments.

Fruity

Bright & lively

Perfect for people who enjoy vibrant coffee with playful, juicy flavor notes.

  • Berry and citrus notes
  • Often lighter roasted
  • Best for curious tasters

Nutty

Comforting & soft

A cozy flavor profile for those who prefer gentle sweetness and smooth balance.

  • Almond, hazelnut, peanut hints
  • Balanced and familiar
  • Great everyday choice

Chocolatey

Rich & warm

Ideal if you enjoy deeper sweetness, smooth body, and dessert-like flavor.

  • Cocoa and caramel notes
  • Fuller body
  • Popular with milk drinks

Bright

Clean & elegant

Great for people who love clarity, crispness, and a fresh finish in the cup.

  • Clean and delicate feel
  • Often floral or tea-like
  • Excellent with pour-over
